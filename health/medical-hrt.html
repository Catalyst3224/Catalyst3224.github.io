Goal

What this section is for

A place to track the medical side of the plan: provider notes, appointment history, what's been tried, what to ask about, and phase-tagged physical milestones as they become relevant. Not a medical record (that lives with providers) — an interpretation layer for your own reference. Helps with continuity of care across providers and with remembering what's been discussed when the gap between appointments is long.

Timeline

Phase-tagged physical milestones

HRT effects unfold over years, not months, and on a personal timeline that's only loosely predictable. Common observations, in rough order:

  • Weeks 1–4 — subjective: mood, libido, fatigue shifts. Often the first noticeable changes.
  • Months 2–6 — skin texture changes, fat redistribution begins, breast budding (tender, sometimes dramatic).
  • Months 6–12 — continued fat redistribution to hips/thighs/chest, reduction in body hair density, slower facial hair growth.
  • Year 1–2 — continued breast development, fuller fat redistribution, softer skin, muscle loss without explicit training.
  • Year 2+ — continued but slower changes. Much of what happens by year 2 is close to what the full trajectory produces.

To be personalized as the actual timeline unfolds. Track observations here so future-you has an actual reference rather than generic literature.

Resources

Trusted information sources

  • WPATH Standards of Care — the canonical clinical guidance document. Useful for knowing what providers should be offering.
  • Trans Care BC — well-organized, lay-accessible information on medical trajectories.
  • Callen-Lorde / Fenway Health — published patient-facing resources from major trans-focused clinics.

Avoid speculation-heavy subreddits for medical decisions — community is valuable for peer support, less so for dosage or protocol questions.
