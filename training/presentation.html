Presentation-adjacent

Heels, purse, skirt, makeup speed

Practical skills that need practice rather than theory. These are the "things girls learned as teenagers and you didn't" — and they have to be learned the same way: by doing them repeatedly until they stop requiring attention.
Overview

What this subsection covers

The physical skills of operating in feminine-coded clothing and accessories. Walking in heels, managing a purse smoothly, managing a skirt across activities, building makeup application speed. Not body language, not posture — practical operational skills.

Prerequisites

Before starting

  • Access to the relevant items — heels, handbag, skirt, makeup — for the applicable area
  • Willingness to practice at home in private before going out
  • Patience — these are more like athletic skills than knowledge; they require hours of practice
Areas

Walking in heels

  • Start with low block heels (1–2"). Platform stilettos (already owned) are more advanced — they reduce effective pitch but require balance.
  • Technique: heel strikes first, then ball of foot rolls down. Don't walk flat-footed.
  • Take smaller steps than in flats. Build up wearing time gradually — an hour at home before trying a full day out.
  • Calf strengthening helps (see Fitness). Weak calves fatigue first in heels.

Purse management

  • Position options: over one shoulder, in crook of elbow (more formal), top-handle in hand, crossbody
  • Access patterns — knowing what's in the purse and where, without visible rummaging
  • Transferring the bag when sitting down — small handbag goes on the floor next to the chair, on the lap, or looped over chair back; not dropped on the table
  • See Accessories for bag specifics

Skirt management

  • Sitting in a skirt — smooth the back of the skirt under with one hand as you sit; knees together or crossed
  • Stairs — going up, skirt sits naturally; going down, one hand holds the front of the skirt slightly if it's voluminous
  • Wind — pressing the skirt down with one hand if wind threatens
  • Getting in and out of cars — see Posture; knees together, swing legs simultaneously

Makeup application speed

  • Starts slow and careful in Phase 3–4 when building skills
  • Target speed by Phase 5: workday routine in 15–20 minutes, special occasion in 30–45
  • Speed comes from repetition and product choice (fewer steps = faster routine)
  • See Makeup for technique
Resources

Where to learn

Free

  • YouTube tutorials on walking in heels — many exist specifically for beginners
  • Observational practice in real settings — notice how women manage their bags, skirts, heels during normal activity

Paid

  • Ballroom or dance classes — teach graceful movement patterns that transfer directly
  • Etiquette coaching — exists as a category; expensive but sometimes covers these skills
Practice structure

How to work on this

  • Heels — practice walking in shoes at home for an hour at a time before wearing them out. Start on carpet, move to hardwood as confidence builds.
  • Purse and skirt management — wear the items around the house. Sit down, stand up, pick things up. The patterns become habitual through doing.
  • Makeup — build speed only after accuracy. Getting it right slowly is better than getting it wrong quickly.
  • One area at a time. Don't try to practice heels, skirt, and purse all in one session.
Milestones

Using the five-stage framework

Each area moves through Introduced → Deliberate → Supported → Default → Integrated independently.

  • Phase 4 — First heels practice, makeup routine speed building. Purse and skirt patterns introduced.
  • Phase 5 — Heels wearable for extended periods, makeup at target speed, purse and skirt management second nature.
  • Phase 6 — Integrated.
