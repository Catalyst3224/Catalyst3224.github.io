Style Icons

Reference figures placed on the dual axes, with what they actually teach

Study their choices for practical direction. The question isn't who you want to look like — it's what specific decisions they make, and which of those translate.

Detail

The figures, individually

Amal Clooney
Elegant · Formal (almost exclusively)

Why she resonates: Impeccably tailored, quietly powerful, never tries too hard.

Key takeaways: Invest in structure. Let quality speak. Avoid trend-chasing. Tailoring does more work than embellishment.

Princess Catherine
Elegant · Formal default, Elegant · Casual when she steps down

Why she resonates: Classic English elegance, always considered and appropriate.

Key takeaways: Clean lines, quality fabrics, occasion-appropriate dressing. Coat-as-outerwear-statement is the signature move.

Anne Hathaway
Ranges across Elegant · Formal and Sexy · Formal; occasional Elegant · Casual

Why she resonates: Romantic with an edge, handles both dramatic and understated.

Key takeaways: Range is possible. Dark and soft can coexist in one wardrobe without incoherence. Willingness to take real aesthetic risks on the red carpet, then pull back to restraint the next day.

France Gall
Elegant · Casual

Why she resonates: 1960s French yé-yé — soft and feminine with melancholy sweetness. The emotional core of the aesthetic.

Key takeaways: Wistful, pretty, sincere. Femininity without performance. Movement and softness over structure. The synthesized aesthetic's "French film from the 1960s" points directly here.

Eva Marie Saint
Elegant · Formal

Why she resonates: Old Hollywood restraint, Grace Kelly adjacent.

Key takeaways: Refinement over revelation. Elegance ages beautifully. Silhouette and fabric quality are doing the work — detail is almost absent.

Ariana Grande
Elegant · Casual home base, ranges freely across all quadrants

Why she resonates: The clearest example of the elegant-casual register done well — oversized hoodie and thigh-high boots is considered, not accidental. Moves into every other quadrant comfortably when the occasion requires.

Key takeaways: Proportion play (oversized top with minimal bottom, or vice versa). Very feminine pieces worn without reading as costume. The high ponytail as a consistent silhouette choice across an entire career. The Positions video is a single-source reference spanning several quadrants; useful as comprehensive inspo rather than just a moment.

Ana de Armas
Sexy · Formal home base, comfortable Elegant · Formal

Why she resonates: Makes sexy-formal look intentional and sophisticated rather than costume-y. Carries the elegant-formal register too — No Time to Die is the textbook example of both operating at once.

Key takeaways: Matching jewelry sets as a complete thought (rather than mixed pieces). Jewelry framing the neckline rather than competing with it. The Chopard set in NTTD is aspirational reference — specific piece unreachable, but the principles (scale, distinctive silhouette, framing over competing) are transferable at any price point. Coherence comes partly from the wearer, but the design decisions are real and separable.

Not on the list

Hindash — technique, not inspo

Hindash's makeup work is genuinely useful as a technique reference — genderless application philosophy, a real eye for cool-toned looks (see Modern Lilac Frost), a working approach to light and shadow. But the aesthetic itself isn't a personal target. Respect for the work without wanting to look like them. This distinction matters for how the Makeup section uses them: Hindash's techniques are transferable, Hindash's overall look isn't the destination.
