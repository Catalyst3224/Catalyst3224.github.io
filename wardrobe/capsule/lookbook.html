Lookbook

Full outfit combinations — the capsule actually working

Same pieces, different combinations. Each outfit is tagged by its dual-axis quadrant so the lookbook serves as a navigation tool when you know the register you need. See Aesthetic Framework for the full explanation of the axes.

Elegant · Formal

Dinner out, theatre, meeting parents, a work event where you want to be taken seriously
Look 01
Elegant · Formal
The French Film
Wool coat over charcoal knit and black silk
  • Fine-knit turtleneck, charcoal
  • Black silk midi skirt
  • Black opaque tights
  • Black Mary Janes
  • Long wool coat, charcoal
  • Silver studs + delicate silver chain
  • Black structured bag, top-handle
The synthesized aesthetic made literal. Every piece in this look is doing its core job — nothing substituted, nothing stretched. This is the outfit to build toward first because once it's assembled, most of the rest of the capsule is already in reach.
Look 02
Elegant · Formal
The Old-Money Lunch
Silk blouse tucked, wide-leg trouser, restraint throughout
  • Ivory silk blouse, notch collar
  • Dark-wash wide-leg trouser
  • Thin black leather belt, silver buckle
  • Black Mary Janes
  • Pearl studs + delicate silver chain
  • Black structured bag
No outerwear required. This is the summer or warm-weather elegant-formal default. Pearls read understated here rather than occasion-y because the rest of the outfit is doing the formality work.
Look 03
Elegant · Formal
The Dark Academia Evening
Forest green silk, charcoal, layered for warmth and weight
  • Forest green silk blouse
  • Black silk midi skirt
  • Black opaque tights
  • Black ankle boots, pointed toe
  • Long wool coat, charcoal
  • Burgundy silk scarf, neck-knot
  • Black structured bag
The literary-academic register at its fullest. Forest green and burgundy are the capsule's two non-neutral colors, paired here without competing because they occupy different jewel-tone registers. Autumn/winter outfit.

Sexy · Formal

A date, a party where you want to make an entrance, a wedding as a guest
Look 04
Sexy · Formal
The Slip Dress Night
Silk, pearl, restrained
  • Black silk slip dress
  • Black Mary Janes (low block heel)
  • Pearl strand or pendant
  • Silver studs or small pearl
  • Black structured bag, top-handle
The Ana de Armas quadrant. The slip dress does the sexy work; pearls and silver restrain it from being overtly revealing. Mary Janes in place of stilettos keeps the outfit elegant rather than predatory — a sophisticated reading of sexy rather than a literal one.
Look 05
Sexy · Formal
The Occasion Full Pull
Platform stilettos, the blacked set, statement belt
  • Black silk slip dress
  • Wider statement belt, burgundy
  • Black gladiator platform stilettos (owned)
  • Pearl strand or bold silver pendant
  • Black structured bag
When the occasion asks for sexy-formal at full volume, the statement belt cinches the slip dress into a more deliberate silhouette, and the platform stilettos from current inventory provide the height and drama. This is the only look that uses existing owned footwear in the elevated register.

Elegant · Casual

Coffee with a friend, a museum, working from somewhere public, errands you actually care about
Look 06
Elegant · Casual
The Campus
Charcoal knit, cream pleated, Stan Smiths
  • Fine-knit turtleneck, charcoal
  • Cream pleated midi skirt
  • Stan Smiths (owned)
  • Silver studs, delicate chain
  • Black structured bag
Light academia at its most wearable. The pleated skirt + sneaker combination is one of the cleanest elegant-casual moves — the skirt provides the literary register, the sneakers keep it from becoming stuffy. Black tights can be added in cold weather without changing the register.
Look 07
Elegant · Casual
The Cardigan Day
Slip dress styled down, cardigan belted, bare-shouldered romance
  • Black silk slip dress
  • Cream oversized cardigan
  • Thin black leather belt (cinches cardigan)
  • Stan Smiths or Mary Janes
  • Pearl studs
Slip dress in elegant-casual mode. The oversized cardigan covers the bare shoulders; belting it over creates the deliberate-looking layered silhouette rather than a slouchy one. Mary Janes pull it toward elegant-formal; sneakers keep it casual. Both work.
Look 08
Elegant · Casual
The Autumn Walk
Knit, jeans, ankle boots, trench as weather allows
  • Cool pink cashmere crewneck
  • Dark-wash wide-leg jean
  • Black ankle boots
  • Cream trench coat (open, belt tied in back)
  • Silk scarf in burgundy
  • Black structured bag, crossbody
The pink knit is the color focal point; everything else is neutral or cool-palette. The trench worn open with the belt tied in back (standard French-girl move) reads casual rather than occupational. Works in every season from September through May depending on layering.
Look 09
Elegant · Casual
The Nap Dress Afternoon
Cream dress, cardigan, sandals or sneakers, minimal jewelry
  • Nap dress, cream
  • Cream oversized cardigan (over shoulders)
  • Stan Smiths
  • Pearl studs
  • Black structured bag or small crossbody
The nap dress's natural habitat. Cream on cream on cream can work precisely because the silhouettes are so different from each other. Pearls close to the ear, nothing at the neck — the dress is already doing the neckline work.

Sexy · Casual

Casual date, a bar, a concert, a house party

Sexy · Casual
The Bar Night
Fitted tee, slip skirt, platform heels, minimal jewelry
  • White fitted tee, tucked
  • Black silk midi skirt
  • Thin black leather belt
  • Platform stilettos (owned)

The tucked-tee-plus-silk-skirt is the quiet-sexy move. The heels do the work; the tee keeps it from becoming formal. Hoops read more sexy-casual than delicate studs in this register — one of the few places where scaling up the jewelry size pays off.
Look 11
Sexy · Casual
The Layered Mesh
Current inventory in elegant-casual styling, elevated
  • Black mesh bodysuit (owned)
  • Dark-wash wide-leg jean or trouser
  • Black ankle boots
  • Cream oversized cardigan (open, over)

Proof that owned pieces don't need to be exiled. The mesh bodysuit reads more deliberate than costume when paired with the wide-leg trouser and cardigan rather than with a mini skirt. This is the bridging outfit — current inventory styled into the capsule's register.

Hybrid moves

Looks that sit between quadrants — where the framework becomes practical
Look 12
Elegant · between Formal and Casual
The Slip as Pinafore
Turtleneck under slip dress — completely different silhouette from the same garment
  • Fine-knit turtleneck, charcoal (under)
  • Black silk slip dress (over)
  • Black opaque tights
  • Black ankle boots or Mary Janes
  • Long wool coat (for outerwear)
  • Pearl studs
The layering move that makes the slip dress a three-season piece. Turtleneck covers chest and arms; slip dress becomes a pinafore over the top. Totally different silhouette, reads literary rather than sexy. Crosses the formal-casual line based entirely on shoe choice.

Building this lookbook

How the lookbook expands

Thirteen looks is a working foundation. The lookbook grows as pieces arrive and new combinations get tested. When a new piece enters the wardrobe, the standard question is which existing pieces can it combine with to produce a look that isn't already represented? If the answer is "none," the piece is a duplicate and doesn't earn its slot. If the answer is "several," it earns a permanent place and those combinations go here.

Pieces from current inventory can appear in looks — the framework doesn't require replacing everything before using anything. Bridge pieces (cowboy boots, Stan Smiths, the mesh bodysuit) do real work in specific quadrants and should be used, not discarded.

Outfits from outside inspo (Ariana's Positions-era looks, Ana de Armas in NTTD, France Gall archival) can be added as reference images in future expansions of this page — image placement and layout TBD when we have images to actually include.
