Shoes

Three shoes cover ninety percent of the capsule's output

Shoes age better than fast-fashion clothing and are worth investing in more intentionally. Three pairs — Mary Janes, clean sneakers, ankle boots — cover everything the capsule wants to do without visual redundancy. The existing platform stilettos stay in the wardrobe as occasion-specific.

Piece 01Black Mary Janes · Low Block Heel or Flat

Closed-toe shoes with a strap across the instep. Mary Janes are the single most versatile shoe in this aesthetic — they work with the midi skirts, the trousers, tights and slip dress, the nap dress. They anchor the literary-academic register more than any other shoe can.

What to look for

  • Pointed or almond toe — round toe reads childish, square toe reads trendy
  • Low block heel (1.5–2") or flat — no stiletto version
  • Single strap with small silver or matte buckle — avoid decorative details
  • Leather or quality vegan leather — avoid anything glossy or plasticky
  • Black is the capsule default; burgundy as a later addition if the shoe shelf grows
Fit note — longer sizing required For genetically-male feet, most women's Mary Jane brands top out at size 11. Brands that extend higher: Nisolo, Everlane, Sam Edelman (up to 12), and specialty unisex/drag lines. Always order the size you know, not the size you want.

Piece 02Minimalist Leather Sneakers · Cream or White

Clean white or cream leather sneakers, minimal branding, low profile. Reads "prep-adjacent" rather than athletic — think tennis club, not running track. Pairs with the wide-leg trouser, jeans, casual pleated skirt. The existing Stan Smiths cover this slot already; this entry is for when they eventually need replacing.

What to look for

  • White or cream leather upper — suede reads different (more casual)
  • Low profile sole — avoid platforms and dad-sneaker chunk
  • Minimal branding — small logo or none
  • Clean laces, white or cream to match
  • Green, navy, or black accent stripe acceptable; multicolor reads too sporty

Piece 03Black Ankle Boots · Pointed Toe, Low Heel

Ankle-height boots — sit just above the ankle bone, not calf-length. Pointed toe, low block or kitten heel (1–2"). Pairs with the wide-leg trouser, jeans, midi skirt with tights. The autumn-winter daily shoe.

What to look for

  • Pointed toe — almond is acceptable, round reads casual
  • Low block heel (1–2") or kitten heel — no flat ankle boots (different shoe entirely)
  • Side zip or elastic gusset — pull-on styles often don't fit cleanly around the ankle
  • Leather upper, clean finish — avoid patent leather (too formal), suede (weather-vulnerable)
  • Minimal hardware — small buckle or none; skip anything western-coded (that's the existing cowboy boots' job)
Existing cowboy boots The cowboy boots in inventory are a bridge piece — they cover the "ankle boot" slot functionally in casual contexts but read western rather than the capsule's register. Continue wearing them in their native aesthetic; add the pointed-toe ankle boot when budget allows for a separate pair that serves the capsule directly.

Summary

Shoes build order

  1. Black Mary Janes — Phase 1–2. Highest-utility shoe, cheap enough to be an early purchase.
  2. Sneakers (Stan Smiths owned) — Phase 2–3 replacement only when current pair wears out.
  3. Black ankle boots — Phase 3–4. Autumn/winter daily shoe.
