Master Plan/ Wardrobe/ Capsule/Accessories

Accessories

Silver hardware throughout — jewelry, scarves, bags, belts, tights

Silver over gold. Pearls as a primary jewelry direction. Belts earn their own entry because a good one elevates a moderately priced outfit more than almost any other accessory. Tights are infrastructure, not jewelry — but without them the skirts don't function in cold weather.

Piece 01Jewelry · Silver + Pearls

The jewelry direction follows directly from the cool undertones and the aesthetic: silver hardware throughout, delicate rather than heavy, and pearls as a primary element. Pearls work specifically well because they're the cool-toned jewelry equivalent of a pearl highlight — soft iridescent white with no warmth. Versatile across all four quadrants of the dual-axis framework in a way very little other jewelry is.

Baseline collection

  • Silver stud earrings — the daily default. Small, delicate, not attention-seeking.
  • Delicate silver chain necklace — one, mid-length. Layering later if desired.
  • Small pearl studs — second earring option. Crosses all registers.
  • Pearl strand or single pendant — occasion piece, anchors sexy-formal or elegant-formal.

Aspirational reference

The Chopard diamond set worn by Ana de Armas in No Time to Die — specifically the matching necklace, earrings, and bracelet. The piece itself is aspirational-to-absurd pricing, but the principles transfer: matching sets as a complete thought (rather than mixed pieces), distinctive silhouette, scale that frames rather than competes with the neckline. See Style Icons for the full treatment. Those principles apply at any price point — a well-chosen cost-tier matching set follows the same logic.

What to avoid

  • Gold or rose gold — fights the cool-undertone direction
  • Costume jewelry that reads plasticky — if it looks fake it is fake
  • Layered chains of different metals — mixed metals read unintentional
  • Statement pieces that compete with the clothing silhouette
  • Anything with visible logos

Piece 02Silk or Silk-Feel Scarf

A square or long silk scarf in a neutral or burgundy colorway. Versatile — hair tie, neck-knot, handbag handle, belt substitute. Reads intentional even when used casually; reads elegant when styled properly. The French-girl accessory.

What to look for

  • 100% silk or washable silk — polyester versions don't drape properly
  • Square scarf (roughly 35") for neck and hair styling; long scarf for more options
  • Colorways: neutral (ivory, cream, muted print) or burgundy for a color moment
  • Subtle pattern acceptable — avoid logos and big brand marks
  • Matte or slight sheen; no high-gloss

Piece 03Black Structured Bag · Mid-size · Crossbody or Top-Handle

A mid-size black bag with structure (holds its shape rather than slouching). Crossbody or top-handle silhouette, not a tote. Reads intentional across every outfit in the capsule. Quality matters here because the bag is the most consistent element of every outfit — it's in every photo, it's carried every day.

What to look for

  • Structured leather or quality vegan leather (no bags that slouch)
  • Mid-size — fits daily essentials without being a full work bag
  • Silver or matte hardware — nothing gold, nothing shiny
  • Crossbody strap detachable and adjustable
  • Minimal branding — small embossed logo or none
  • Interior organization — one zip pocket minimum
Why structured over slouchy The capsule's aesthetic leans literary/old-money/restrained. Slouchy bags read bohemian or casual-maximalist. A structured bag carries the "considered" quality of the wardrobe more consistently across every register.

Piece 04Belts — Two Pieces

Belts are among the highest-leverage accessories: they define waist, break up monochrome outfits, and add a focal point without changing the core pieces. Two belts cover most of the capsule's needs — a thin workhorse and a wider statement belt. Expensive belts elevate moderately-priced dresses and trousers; this is the one accessory category where investing slightly more than seems reasonable pays off.

Thin black leather belt with silver hardware

The workhorse. Cinches dresses, defines the waist on trousers, breaks up the visual weight of the oversized cardigan. This is the version that goes with everything.

  • Full-grain leather, no painted edges
  • Silver buckle, small and flat — avoid decorative shapes
  • 3/4" to 1" width
  • Multiple sizing holes for weight change

Wider statement belt · Black or burgundy leather

The investment piece. Elevates moderately-priced dresses and trousers; is itself the focal point rather than a support element. Isabel Marant-style wide leather with distinctive buckle, or vintage for distinctive pieces. Burgundy is the capsule's color statement in this slot.

  • 2–3" width
  • Substantial buckle — brass, silver, or tonal leather
  • Fits at the natural waist rather than hip
  • Leather holds shape (doesn't roll or curl)

Piece 05Black Opaque Tights — Infrastructure

Not jewelry. Not decoration. Structural. Without good opaque tights, the midi skirts and slip dress don't function in cold weather — which is most of the year in Ohio. Treat these as consumables; buy multiple pairs at once because they will ladder and you don't want to be caught without a backup.

What to look for

  • True opaque — 60+ denier (lower denier shows skin through)
  • Matte finish, no visible sheen
  • Reinforced toe and heel
  • Control-top or no-control-top — preference, not quality indicator
  • Black only — other colors in this register read costume
Keep spares Buy four pairs at once. Rotate them. When one ladders, throw it out and buy the replacement immediately. A missing tights pair on a cold morning undoes half the wardrobe.

Summary

Accessories build order

  1. Black opaque tights (four pairs) — Phase 1–2. Infrastructure.
  2. Silver studs + delicate chain + pearl studs — Phase 2–3. Baseline jewelry.
  3. Thin black leather belt — Phase 3. Workhorse accessory.
  4. Silk scarf, black structured bag — Phase 3–4. Elevation.
  5. Wider statement belt (black or burgundy) — Phase 4. Investment accessory.
  6. Pearl strand / statement pendant — Phase 5–6. Occasion.
