Bottoms

Three pieces that cover the full range — black silk, dark wide-leg, cream pleated

The black silk skirt is the single most versatile piece in the capsule. Build order puts it first among bottoms for that reason — the wide-leg and cream pleated both extend the range the skirt establishes.

Piece 01Black Silk Midi Skirt

Bias-cut, hits mid-calf, flows rather than holds shape. Pairs with every top in the capsule to produce different aesthetic registers: sleek Parisian with the turtleneck, old-money polish with the ivory silk blouse, soft-romantic with the cashmere crewneck.

What to look for

  • Bias cut (drapes on the diagonal) rather than straight cut
  • Elastic or hidden elastic waist — forgiving through body recomposition
  • Hem hits mid-calf (not knee, not ankle)
  • Fully opaque — check reviews specifically; some silks are sheer
  • Matte or slight sheen, not high-gloss

Piece 02Dark-Wash Wide-Leg Trouser or High-Waist Jean

The counterweight to the skirts. Wide-leg pant silhouette provides structure and drama without becoming rigid — a key piece for the elegant-casual register that the capsule centers on. The current skinny jeans (comfort-confirmed, kept as a bridge piece) are eventually replaced by this.

What to look for

  • Dark wash — no distressing, no fading effects. Clean indigo or black.
  • High-waist — hits at the natural waist, tucks in cleanly with the silk blouse or turtleneck
  • Wide straight leg from hip — not flared, not tapered
  • Length hits at the ankle or slightly above depending on the shoe
  • Heavyweight fabric — holds its shape rather than sagging
Trouser vs. jean — which first The trouser version is more versatile for the elegant register — tucked-silk-blouse styling reads old-money in a way that jeans simply can't. The jean version is more casual and everyday. If buying one first, trouser; if you need casual-forward over polished, jean.

Piece 03Cream or Oatmeal Pleated Midi Skirt

The tenniscore / prep anchor. Pleated construction — vertical permanent pleats from waist to hem, moving fluidly. In cream or oatmeal, this piece moves the capsule into the light-academia and soft-prep territories that aren't well served by the black skirt alone.

What to look for

  • Knife pleats (narrow, tight) rather than box pleats — reads more sophisticated
  • Mid-calf length, matching the black skirt
  • Lightweight fabric that holds pleats — polyester or wool-blend
  • Cream or oatmeal (warm-cream but leaning neutral); avoid bright white and warm yellow-cream
  • Elastic or hidden zipper waist
Cream vs. oatmeal Either pairs beautifully with the charcoal turtleneck, the ivory silk blouse, and the cashmere crewneck. For cool undertones, oatmeal reads slightly better than true cream near the face. This one's a soft preference, not a rule.

Summary

Bottoms build order

  1. Black silk midi skirt — Phase 2–3. The most versatile bottom.
  2. Wide-leg trouser or jean — Phase 3–4. Replaces skinny jean as the daily non-skirt option.
  3. Cream pleated midi — Phase 4–5. Light-academia expansion, lowest-urgency bottom.
