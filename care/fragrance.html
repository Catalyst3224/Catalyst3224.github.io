Fragrance

From masculine foundations toward unisex and feminine

The existing collection is not a starting-from-zero situation — it's a thoughtfully built set of classic aromatic fougères, mossy chypres, and clean elegance. The direction going forward is expansion, not replacement. Unisex territory first, softer florals second, full-bottle commitment only after sampling.
Goal

What the fragrance wardrobe is becoming

A wardrobe rather than a signature scent. Different fragrances for different contexts, moods, and seasons — the same logic as clothing. The existing collection leans masculine-traditional (aromatic fougères, chypres, green). The expansion adds unisex and softly feminine options, building toward a collection that ranges across the dual-axis framework the wardrobe uses. Wear what fits the day and the register.

Direction

Where the collection is headed

The goal isn't departing from the current collection — it's expanding into territory the masculine-traditional picks don't cover. The /fa/ fragrance charts are a useful guide for understanding the landscape but miss that "feminine-coded" just means "further from masculine center," not "intrinsically gendered." Plenty of fragrances labeled feminine sit closer to the aesthetic direction of the wardrobe than many labeled masculine.

Three directions to explore, in rough order of distance from current taste:

  1. Unisex with structure — Replica line (Maison Margiela), some Le Labo. Bridges from the current taste with minimal jump.
  2. Complex florals and white florals — Serge Lutens, Dusita, Amouage. More distinct from current collection but still complex and niche-serious.
  3. Overtly feminine with personality — Frederic Malle, Ex Nihilo, certain Chanel. The furthest from starting point.
Sampling wishlist

Phase 4 — Scent Split order

Decants rather than full bottles. Scent Split and Try Fragrance sell small portions (1ml, 5ml, 10ml) cheaply. Total cost for the whole shortlist is typically $40–60, compared to $500+ for full bottles — and worst-case for sampling is "I don't love it," not a $150 bottle you don't reach for.

Order of sampling — not all at once Try them on skin, one at a time, with at least a day between. White florals especially change dramatically on skin and need space to develop without olfactory fatigue muddying judgment. A solid arc: Replica Flower Market first → Reflection Man → Lys 41 → At the Barber's → Mélodie de l'Amour → Lipstick Rose → Tuberose Criminelle. Accessible to challenging.
What to skip

Not the direction

  • Muscs Koublai Khan (Serge Lutens) — aggressively animalic, "unwashed skin" profile. Too far in the opposite direction for where the collection is heading.
  • Prada L'Homme — firmly in the clean aromatic masculine lane, very similar DNA to existing YSL L'Homme. No new territory.
  • Heavy gourmands (Thierry Mugler Angel, Prada Candy) — sweet/dessert fragrance; different aesthetic register entirely.
  • Celebrity fragrances — generally not distinctive enough to belong in this collection.
Resources

Where to sample and buy

  • Scent Split — primary decanting service. Wide catalog, reliable quality, small sample sizes affordable.
  • Try Fragrance — alternative to Scent Split. Different catalog; worth checking both for specific fragrances.
  • Fragrancenet, FragranceX — discounted full bottles if a sample leads to commitment. Always check these before paying retail.
  • Profumo.it — direct from Dominique Dubrana. Already familiar.
  • Luckyscent, Twisted Lily — niche boutiques; often carry samples of hard-to-find pieces directly.
Application

Technique notes

  • Spray on skin (pulse points — wrist, neck) for true development. Paper strips give early impression but not the full arc.
  • Don't rub wrists together after applying — breaks up top notes unnecessarily.
  • Less than you think. Fragrance projects more to others than to yourself (you acclimate).
  • Moisturized skin holds fragrance longer than dry skin.
  • Different fragrances for different seasons — heavier in cold weather, lighter in warm.
  • Signature-scent thinking is optional. A wardrobe lets context drive choice.
