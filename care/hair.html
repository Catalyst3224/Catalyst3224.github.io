Hair

Monday/Thursday wash rotation, with co-washing for off days

Natural hair is dark, defined curls with good texture, currently growing out. The routine alternates Head & Shoulders (dandruff control) with Shea Moisture (moisture recovery) to avoid over-drying from antifungal shampoo while still keeping dandruff suppressed.
Goal

What the routine is doing

Two jobs: control the dandruff (persistent concern), and support curl health through growth. Head & Shoulders works for dandruff but is drying — alternating with a moisturizing shampoo keeps the curls from becoming crispy. The whole routine is tuned for defined curls; if hair texture were different, the product choices would be too.

Wash technique (both days)

  1. Wet hair thoroughly
  2. Scalp massager with shampoo — let sit 1–2 minutes
  3. Conditioner mid-length to ends — leave a few minutes
  4. Rinse with cooler water (seals cuticle, reduces frizz)
  5. Microfiber towel — blot and scrunch, never rub
  6. Air dry; apply leave-in conditioner on ends once dry
Co-washing

For sweaty non-wash days

On off days when hair gets sweaty from work or activity, the right move is neither a full wash (resets the wash day clock, over-cleanses) nor doing nothing (sweat sits and causes buildup and irritation). Instead:

  • Co-wash (conditioner-only wash) — massage conditioner into scalp like shampoo, rinse thoroughly. Clears sweat and odor without stripping moisture. Doesn't reset the wash day clock.
  • Water rinse with scalp massage — alternative for quick situations. Less thorough but fine occasionally.
  • Dry shampoo — for roots only, not a solution for full-hair sweat. Useful in a pinch.
How often to co-wash As needed. If sweaty work days happen 2–3 times a week, co-washing on those days is fine. Don't co-wash if hair isn't actually dirty — conditioner on already-clean hair just weighs it down.
Phased additions

What gets added when

  • Phase 1 — Base wash rotation, microfiber towel, leave-in conditioner.
  • Phase 2 — Pre-wash scalp oil treatment (jojoba or rosehip) 30–60 min before Monday's wash. Stays on during the shampoo step.
  • Phase 3 — Deep conditioning hair mask 1–2× per month (Shea Moisture Manuka Honey or similar). Replaces regular conditioner that wash.
  • Phase 4 — Diffuser attachment for hair dryer. Transforms fine curly hair without frizz. Learn to diffuse rather than rough-dry.
  • Phases 4+ — Basic style practice: half-up, simple braids, soft waves with diffuser. Reference: Asuka Kirara's soft short-to-medium aesthetic.
Technique

Handling curly hair properly

  • Never brush dry curls — breaks the curl pattern and causes frizz. Detangle with a wide-tooth comb or fingers while wet with conditioner.
  • Scrunch, don't rub — cotton towels create friction that breaks curls; microfiber is gentler. Scrunch upward toward the scalp to encourage curl formation.
  • Avoid touching while drying — touching wet curls mid-dry causes frizz. Hands off once product is applied until fully dry.
  • Sleep on silk or satin — cotton pillowcases create friction. A silk pillowcase or satin bonnet preserves curl overnight.
  • Less product than you think — easier to add than to remove. Start small.
Scalp oil treatments

Pre-wash, phased in

Apply jojoba or rosehip oil to scalp 30–60 minutes before washing. Massage in gently. The shampoo then lifts both the oil and whatever it has loosened. Moisturizes the scalp without needing separate scalp-targeted products.

  • Jojoba — closest to natural sebum. Lightweight, doesn't feel heavy. The default choice.
  • Rosehip — slightly heavier, nourishing. Good for particularly dry scalps or winter months.
  • Avoid coconut oil for this purpose — too heavy, can clog follicles on some scalps.
