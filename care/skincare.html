Skincare

CeraVe baseline, phased addition of actives

Skin quality is downstream of consistency. The baseline cleanser-moisturizer routine is non-negotiable; actives (vitamin C, retinol) are additive once the habit is there. Don't add everything at once — skin tolerance builds gradually, and so does the habit.
Goal

What this routine is for

Even skin tone, soft texture, calm barrier function. Not aggressive anti-aging, not a 12-step Korean routine. Simple and consistent beats complex and abandoned. The routine as designed also accommodates the three specific skin concerns identified: dandruff (addressed under Hair), keratosis pilaris on arms/legs, and care of the pilonidal site.

Daily routine

Morning

  • CeraVe facial cleanser
  • Gentle toner (witch hazel or hydrating)
  • Vitamin C serum — pat, let absorb
  • CeraVe moisturizer on face and dry areas
  • Facial sunscreen if going outside (CeraVe AM or EltaMD)
  • Lip balm
  • Cuticle oil on fingers

Evening

  • CeraVe facial cleanser
  • Gentle toner
  • Retinol (starting 2×/week, building up)
  • CeraVe moisturizer
  • Eye cream if desired — pat gently, never drag
  • Cuticle oil
  • Tucks pad if needed

Every shower

  • Shower cap on non-hair-wash days
  • Dove Sensitive with silicone scrubber
  • Be gentle on pilonidal site — no harsh scrubbing
  • AmLactin on KP areas immediately after on damp skin
Theory

Why these products

  • CeraVe — ceramide-based, repairs skin barrier rather than just cleaning. Suits sensitive or reactive skin. Budget-friendly and consistent.
  • AmLactin — lactic acid at 12%. Works on KP (keratosis pilaris) by exfoliating the bumps. Takes weeks to show results; consistency matters more than intensity.
  • Tucks pads — witch hazel for pilonidal site care. Gentle, antiseptic, reduces irritation.
  • Vitamin C — antioxidant, brightens tone over time, protects against UV damage. Takes 8–12 weeks to show meaningful change.
  • Retinol — the most effective anti-aging active. Also improves texture and evens tone. Starts mild (once or twice a week), builds up slowly. Skin needs to tolerate it before daily use.
The mistake to avoid Adding multiple actives at once. Every new product should get at least two weeks alone before stacking. If skin reacts badly, you need to know which product caused it. Patch-test new products on the inner forearm first.
Phased additions

When to add what

  • Phase 1 (Months 1–2) — CeraVe cleanser and moisturizer baseline. AmLactin for KP. Tucks as needed. Consistency is the goal — nothing fancy.
  • Phase 2 (Months 2–4) — Add toner morning and evening. Add facial sunscreen for mornings when going outside.
  • Phase 3 (Months 4–6) — Add vitamin C serum in the morning. Add eye cream if desired.
  • Phase 4 (Months 6–9) — Add retinol evenings, 2×/week initially. Build to 3–4×/week once skin tolerates. Never use retinol and vitamin C in the same application window.
  • Phases 5–6 — Routine stable. Occasional additions based on specific concerns (hyperpigmentation, fine lines) can happen but aren't pushed.

Technique notes

How to apply

  • Cleansing — lukewarm water, gentle circular motion. Don't scrub. Pat dry with a clean towel.
  • Toner — pat into skin with hands or soft cotton pad. Don't rub.
  • Serum — small amount, press into skin. Wait 30–60 seconds for absorption before next step.
  • Moisturizer — apply while skin is still slightly damp for better absorption.
  • Retinol — pea-sized amount for entire face. Avoid eye area immediately (move closer only after skin adjusts). Moisturize on top.
  • Eye cream — ring finger, small dot, pat gently from outer corner inward. Never drag.
  • Sunscreen — generous application. Two fingers' length worth of product for face and neck. Reapply every 2 hours if outside.
Tools

Equipment

  • Silicone body scrubber — gentle, doesn't harbor bacteria like loofahs. Already owned.
  • Vibrating silicone face scrubber — optional, already owned. Use 2–3×/week, not daily. Heat setting can help with absorption; avoid on active breakouts.
  • Clean washcloths — fresh one daily if possible. Bacteria on reused towels can cause breakouts.
